Recipe No. 13 —

Family Meal Fried Rice

Time 45min
Serves 6-8
Season Any
Difficulty Unique

Why this one

This is something that has remained in my cycle of life for ages. This recipe doesn't get old, it seems to get younger and more delicious and fresh with time. It is one that's better shared than kept— like so many things in life.

If you want something you can eat Breakfast, Lunch or Dinner, this is easy, friendly, and all its own style. It's welcoming to whatever you like to put in it, but over the last years this has been my reoccurring combination. I preferred things freshly made, but this serves those times you are drawn to something just as great— when it's leftover.

Ingredients

5 cups Freshly (or left over) cooked White Rice
1 bunch Scallion sliced
3-5 eggs scrambled
1 bag bean sprouts
2 cups chopped carrots steamed carrots
1 cup golden raisins
1/4 cup coconut aminos
1/4 cup soy sauce or (soy sauce powder)
1 Tbs Garlic Powder

The Oil

1/2 cup+ Avocado or high heat oil
2 Tbls Sesame Oil — mixed in with the above

Method

01

If your rice is cooked fresh, let it air out a bit using a spatula to "cut it" and create space for air to hit the rice. You can spread it on a baking sheet and drizzle the oil on it. Or if it's already been cooked, oil it in the refrigerator uncovered overnight. You don't have to do this, but giving time to let the moisture out of the rice will introduce something new.

02

Next, blanch your diced carrots in boiling water and strain them, or steam them in the rice cooker with the rice if you have a steam basket.

03

For most of us who don't have a wok and burners that cook over 500 degrees, use a large non-stick pan on high heat and cook off your bean sprouts. Set aside.

04

Toast your steamed carrots as well and set aside with sprouts.

05

Add a little of the same oil you tossed the rice in to the same pan, just enough to coat the pan, since your rice will already have some oil as well. Add the rice and cook on medium heat adjusting as needed for your stove. You want the rice browning, but not burning.

06

Cook the rice using a spatula to lightly turn, fold and brown for 10-15minutes or more. Giving it time to sit and crisp before each turn and fold. As it begins to brown, add your garlic powder and soy sauce and coconut aminos.

07

Add the golden raisins and stir for 5 minutes.

08

You can cook your egg in a separate pan and add it to the rice with everything else now, or just add the rice to a large bowl with your scallion, sprouts, carrots, and use the same pan to cook your eggs and fold it into the rice.

Notes

An Extra Ingredient & Step

You didn't miss or forget but may just find making its way to you: Chopped or picked cilantro if you have it growing just may be something nice at the end.
