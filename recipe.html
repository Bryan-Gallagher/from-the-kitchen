Slow Cook
Recipe No. 02

Braised Short Ribs with Red Wine

Time 3 hrs
Serves 6
Season Winter
Difficulty Slow

Why this one

I was walking back from the store. A man was sitting outside the parking garage — not asking for anything, just sitting. It was cold enough that I could see his breath.

I didn't know what to do with that. So I went home and started cooking. Not for him — I didn't have the courage for that. For the feeling of doing something with your hands when everything else feels impossible.

I think about him when I make this. Cooking holds things. The things you couldn't say. You put them in the pot and you let them go slow.

Ingredients

The ribs

3 lbs bone-in short ribs English cut
2 tsp kosher salt
1 tsp black pepper, coarse
2 tbsp neutral oil

The braise

1 onion, roughly chopped
4 garlic cloves, crushed
3 carrots, roughly chopped
2 celery stalks
2 tbsp tomato paste
1 bottle red wine something drinkable
2 cups beef stock
4 thyme sprigs
2 bay leaves

Method

01

Season the ribs generously with salt and pepper. Let them sit at room temperature for 30 minutes — this matters more than most steps.

02

Heat the oil in a heavy pot until it shimmers. Brown the ribs in batches — don't crowd them. You want deep colour on all sides. This takes patience. Maybe 12 minutes per batch. Set aside.

03

In the same pot, cook the onion and carrots until soft and starting to colour, about 8 minutes. Add the garlic, cook another minute. Add the tomato paste and let it cook into the vegetables for 2 minutes — it should darken slightly.

04

Pour in the wine. Scrape up everything from the bottom of the pot. Let it reduce by half — about 15 minutes. The smell will change. This is good.

05

Add the stock, thyme, and bay leaves. Nestle the ribs back in — they should be mostly submerged. Bring to a simmer, then cover and cook on the lowest possible heat for 2½ hours. Or transfer to a 325°F oven.

06

The ribs are done when a fork slides through with no resistance. Let them rest in the liquid for at least 20 minutes before serving. Skim the fat. Taste the braising liquid — reduce it if you want something more concentrated.

Notes

Make ahead

Better the next day. Refrigerate overnight — the fat solidifies on top and lifts off cleanly. Reheat gently, covered.

To serve

Over polenta, mashed potatoes, or just with bread to catch the sauce. Gremolata on top if you want brightness. It doesn't need it.

The wine

Use something you'd drink. Nothing expensive — the cooking will change it. A Côtes du Rhône or any sturdy red works well.

Who this is for

Anyone who needs to feel taken care of. Including yourself.
