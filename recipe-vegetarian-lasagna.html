Vegetarian Lasagna

Time 90–120 min
Serves 12
Season Fall
Difficulty Fun

Why this one

You may find certain times in your life ask for different ways of eating. This dish is that. How you prepare it is up to you. I love cheese, but sometimes the body asks for a change, and vegan ricotta is a nice change of pace.

The layers of time always come through in a nice lasagna. However you make it, Marinara wants to be invited.

Ingredients

For the Build

3 zucchini, sliced on a mandolin lengthwise or in circles
3 yellow squash, sliced on a mandolin lengthwise or in circles
6 large portabella mushrooms, seared or roasted beforehand — sliced or whole
3 cups mozzarella, preferably Grande — or your favorite vegan mozzarella
½ cup fresh basil, chopped or torn
8 cups Marinara (about 2 mason jars)
— Ricotta mixture, see below
— Béchamel, optional

Ricotta Mixture

1–3 lbs ricotta or vegan ricotta
1 per lb egg, if making vegetarian
¼ cup per lb Parmigiano Reggiano or Pecorino, grated
½ tbsp per lb granulated garlic
— fresh parsley, chopped — as much as you like
1 tbsp kosher salt

Method

—

Before You Start: Let the Oven Warm Up to You... Heat Oven to 425

01

Decide if you want to mandoline your zucchini and squash, cut them in circles.

02

The portabella you will want to grill or sear with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Then you can slice them or leave them whole.

03

In a Pyrex (or other baking) pan, put a little sauce in the bottom just to coat it.

04

Lay down a layer of zucchini or a layer of squash, or alternate them to cover the bottom of the pan after you decide if you want layers of color or everything mixed together.

05

Add some sauce and then layer in spinach and your portabella.

06

Coat with sauce once again and add another layer of your preferred squash.

07

Now, cover this layer with your ricotta mixture.

08

Add your remaining squash. And a drizzle of olive oil.

—

Extra Step: If using more ricotta, repeat the above steps. You can also play with how you layer it.

09

If you're going to serve it right out of the oven, I suggest adding the marinara on top or the bechamel if using it. Then coat with your mozzarella. If serving it later, you can bake it with olive oil and add your toppings later on the second bake.

—

Something Between Us: If you wrap your pan with Parchment paper, then plastic wrap, then foil. It will actually hold all the steam in, and cook everything super evenly. Especially if you have the cheese on top, it will melt beautifully. Then you would remove it to brown the cheese at the end.

10

Bake it for about 45 minutes to an Hour in your preheated oven. Feel it with a knife if you're not sure about the cook.

11

Time for service. You likely want to let it rest for about 10-15 minutes or so if you're serving it fresh. You can add fresh basil to the top, or left over fresh parsley. A little more olive oil and cheese if you like. Cut it and serve it.

For Another Day

If you're serving it the next day, peel open any wrapping you have on it and let it cool enough to put it in the fridge. Wrapped up. After it's chilled, you can cut it into portions that you want. Then when you're ready to serve, put your marinara or bechamel on top with the cheese and bake it to warm it up. And it's ready to go.

Notes

Make Ahead

This can be made a day or two ahead — the flavors marry together and it's honestly even better. It's also wonderful right out of the oven. Both are true.

Going Vegan

Try a very low temperature bake so the vegetables are warmed but not fully cooked through — a different kind of freshness that works beautifully with vegan ricotta.

The Cut

In the picture, everything is cut in circles — rustic and full of character. For a more refined lasagna, use a mandolin or cut thick, even slices so they layer like pasta sheets. Either way is right.

Layered with Love

What you need at the time can determine how decadent or light you make this. Any lasagna is a dish layered with love. Honored in time. Ask yourself — how much did you enjoy making this?
