From Scratch
Recipe No. 2 —

The Marinara

Time 60 Min
Serves 10
Season All
Difficulty Easy

Why this one

For its simple beauty.

Ingredients

All You Need

1 can Alta Cucina Whole Peeled Tomatoes (#10 can)
6–10 Cloves of garlic, pressed
2 Small sweet or yellow onions, small dice
½ cup+ Olive oil (maybe more)
1 cup Fresh basil, chopped or torn
— Salt & pepper to taste

Method

01

Before you start — Use a pot deep enough to hold twice the tomatoes so you have room to stir and break them up without making a mess. Unless you enjoy being messy. Put it on a burner over low-medium heat and warm half your olive oil.

02

Add your pressed garlic to the warm oil. You could mince it, but dare to press it. Stir with a wooden spoon and let the fragrance really have a good time with you.

03

As the garlic transforms into golden essence, add the diced onions. Add the other half of the oil if it seems like it needs it. This oil is the magic — the medium through which the garlic, onions, and tomatoes will eventually marry.

04

As the onions become translucent, it's their message to the tomatoes: Come in, please. Add the whole peeled tomatoes. They can be any good brand, but they are truly better off being Alta Cucina. I can't tell you exactly why — just discover it for yourself.

05

Turn the heat to medium-high and take a whisk. Stir periodically. As the sauce begins to simmer, bring the heat back to medium and let time and your whisking do the work.

06

Use the whisk to gently mash and break up the tomatoes as you stir. You'll do this for 30–60 minutes. Watch it come together.

07

There's gotta be more to it, right? You really can't add too much olive oil — a little more now never hurts. A pinch of salt and pepper. Then turn off the heat and fold in the basil with the whisk. At least one cup. And you're done.

Notes

Make a double batch

Sometimes I'll make a double batch and pour it hot into large mason jars. Seal them while hot and let cool to room temperature on the counter. Follow long-practiced canning methods — many suggest a dash of citrus or citric acid powder to preserve in the pantry for up to a year. Or skip the citrus and keep it sealed in the refrigerator for months, just fine.

On freshness

Just when you think something is always one way, dishes like this remind us fresh isn't always what you imagine it to be. May this refresh your day into something newly discovered.
